## Book Summaries

### [Rework](./rework)

> Rework is a business book that offers unconventional and practical advice for starting and running a successful business. It encourages readers to question traditional workplace practices and focus on simplicity, efficiency, and genuine customer service. The book is broken down into micro-chapters — each between 2–3 pages long and containing a single tip designed to show how the authors have bucked convention.

### [The defining decade](the-defining-decade)

> Your twenties are the most transformative — and most wasted — decade of your life. Society tells young adults to treat their twenties as a free pass, a consequence-free intermission before "real life" begins. Meg Jay argues the opposite: **80% of life's most defining moments happen by age 35**, meaning the twenties are not a waiting room but a launching pad.

### [The Lean Startup](the-lean-startup)

> Most startups fail — not because they build something *bad*, but because they build something *nobody wants*. Traditional business planning is fiction dressed as strategy. Eric Ries argues that startups need a **scientific approach to innovation**: test assumptions rapidly, learn from real customers, and adapt before running out of time and money.
